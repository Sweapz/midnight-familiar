# GAME_VISION

## One-Sentence Pitch
Midnight Familiar is a monster-collection tactical battler where the player collects, trains, and commands Cuids in strategic, dice-driven combat in a modern-fantasy world with grounded but magical undertones.

## Player Fantasy
- You are a Cuid handler and strategist navigating a hidden magical layer of the modern world.
- You feel clever, tactical, and in control.
- Your companion/familiar fantasy is building a team of unique Cuids whose traits and abilities create powerful synergies and playstyles.

## Design Pillars
1. Tactical Depth Through Simplicity: Combat is easy to understand but offers deep decision-making through positioning, abilities, and team composition.
2. Every Cuid Feels Unique: Traits, types, and stat distributions create meaningful differences—even between similar Cuids.
3. Player-Driven Strategy: Success comes from planning, synergy, and adaptation—not just raw stats or grinding.

## Core Loop
1. Enter zone / city / encounter.
2. Explore and encounter new Cuids or trainers.
3. Engage in tactical combat (4v4).
4. Gain new Cuids, resources, or upgrades.
5. Prepare and repeat with new options.

## Player Verbs
- Move: Grid-based movement influenced by Speed (TBD exact system).
- Attack/Cast: Use direct attacks or abilities, resolved via dice rolls (d20-based).
- Defend/Evade: Avoid or mitigate incoming actions through Defense and positioning.
- Command Familiar: Select actions for each of your 4 Cuids, leveraging traits and synergy.
- Harvest/Collect: Capture or obtain new Cuids and resources in the overworld.

## Familiar System (Core Concept)
- Familiar types: Elemental or categorical types that determine strengths/weaknesses (TBD exact type list).
- Bond/progression: Cuids grow stronger through use, leveling (TBD depth).
- Combat utility: Each Cuid contributes via 4 selectable actions, stats, and a defining trait.
- Exploration utility: TBD (potentially traversal, detection, or overworld interactions).
- Tradeoff: Limited team size (4 Cuids) and action slots force meaningful choices.

## Progression
- Short-term (per run/session): Gain new Cuids, experience, and resources.
- Mid-term (between runs): Build and optimize teams, unlock new Cuids or abilities (TBD structure).
- Long-term (account/save): Expand collection, unlock regions, and deepen strategic options (TBD).

## World and Tone
- Setting: A modern world layered with hidden magical elements where Cuids exist alongside everyday life..
- Mood words: Mysterious, strategic, grounded, magical.
- Narrative style: TBD (likely light narrative with focus on gameplay systems).
- Visual direction: Modern environments with subtle magical overlays; creature-focused design (TBD specifics).
- Audio direction: Tactical, slightly tense combat music with clean, readable SFX.

## Target Audience and Platform
- Audience: Players who enjoy monster collection and tactical combat (e.g., Pokémon + DnD fans).
- Platform(s): PC.
- Session length target: 20–40 minutes.
- Input assumptions: keyboard+mouse.

## Scope Guardrails
- Must-have for v0.1: 
    - 4v4 combat system with initiative and d20 resolution
    - A small pool of Cuids (5–10)
    - Basic overworld navigation between locations
    - Core stat system and abilities implemented
- Nice-to-have later:.
    - Expanded type system
    - Trait variety and deeper synergies
    - Exploration abilities for Cuids
    - Narrative elements
- Explicit non-goals: TBD.

## Success Criteria
- The game is successful if players can build a team of Cuids and feel rewarded for strategic decisions in combat.
- First-play test should show players understanding turn order, using abilities, and experimenting with team composition.
- Retention signal to watch: players wanting to collect more Cuids and refine their team strategy.

## Current Decisions (Canon)
- 2026-03-18: Combat will be 4v4 and inspired by DnD 5e using d20 rolls to determine outcomes.
- 2026-03-18: Cuids have 5 base stats and 5 secondary stats, plus types and traits for differentiation
- 2026-03-18: Combat will be prototyped on an 8x8 square grid to support tactical positioning and clear readability. A hex grid remains a possible future experiment.
